01

Each Adverb in Detail

always
100%
siempre — every single time, no exceptions
I always brush my teeth before bed.
Siempre me lavo los dientes antes de dormir.
She always arrives on time.
Ella siempre llega a tiempo.
We always have coffee in the morning.
Siempre tomamos café en la mañana.
It always rains in April here.
Aquí siempre llueve en abril.
usually
80–90%
generalmente — most of the time
I usually wake up at 7am.
Generalmente me despierto a las 7am.
He usually takes the metro to work.
Él normalmente toma el metro al trabajo.
We usually eat dinner at 7pm.
Generalmente cenamos a las 7pm.
She usually checks her phone first thing.
Ella normalmente revisa el celular lo primero.
often
60–70%
frecuentemente — many times, regularly
I often go for a walk after lunch.
Frecuentemente salgo a caminar después del almuerzo.
They often have meetings on Mondays.
Ellos frecuentemente tienen reuniones los lunes.
Do you often cook at home?
¿Cocinas en casa con frecuencia?
He often forgets his keys. 😄
Él frecuentemente olvida sus llaves.
sometimes
30–50%
a veces — not regularly, but it happens
I sometimes work from home.
A veces trabajo desde casa.
She sometimes skips breakfast.
Ella a veces se salta el desayuno.
We sometimes order pizza on Fridays.
A veces pedimos pizza los viernes.
Sometimes I feel like staying home.
A veces siento quedarme en casa. (inicio)

10–20%

I rarely eat fast food.
Rara vez como comida rápida.

Él rara vez va al gimnasio ya.

0%

02

Where to Put Them — Word Order

The position of frequency adverbs is very specific. Get it wrong and the sentence sounds unnatural. Here are the rules:

✓ Rule 1 — Before the main verb
Subject + [adverb] + main verb
I always eat breakfast.
Siempre desayuno.
Subject + [adverb] + main verb
She usually works late.
Ella generalmente trabaja tarde.
Subject + [adverb] + main verb
They often go hiking.
Ellos frecuentemente van de excursión.
Subject + [adverb] + main verb
He never drinks alcohol.
Él nunca toma alcohol.
✓ Rule 2 — After "to be"
Subject + to be + [adverb]
She is always late!
¡Ella siempre llega tarde!
Subject + to be + [adverb]
He is usually very kind.
Él generalmente es muy amable.
Subject + to be + [adverb]
The meetings are rarely short.
Las reuniones rara vez son cortas.
Subject + to be + [adverb]
The office is never empty.
La oficina nunca está vacía.
✓ Rule 3 — After auxiliary verbs
Subject + aux + [adverb] + verb
I have always loved music.
Siempre he amado la música.
Subject + aux + [adverb] + verb
You should always check the details.
Siempre deberías revisar los detalles.
Subject + aux + [adverb] + verb
She can often work from home.
Ella frecuentemente puede trabajar desde casa.
Subject + aux + [adverb] + verb
I would never say that.
Nunca diría eso.
✓ Rule 4 — Sometimes at the start or end
[Sometimes] + Subject + verb (flexible!)
Sometimes I just want to stay home.
A veces solo quiero quedarme en casa.
Subject + verb + [sometimes] (end)
I eat out sometimes.
A veces como afuera.
[Often/usually] can move to start too
Often, the best ideas come at night.
Frecuentemente, las mejores ideas llegan de noche.
⚠ NEVER and RARELY — middle only!
I never eat there. ✓  /  Never I eat ✗
Never y rarely NO van al inicio de la oración.
03

More Frequency Expressions

Beyond the 6 main adverbs, English has many other ways to express frequency — these usually go at the end of the sentence:

every day
todos los días
I exercise every day.
every week
cada semana
She calls me every week.
once a week
una vez a la semana
I go to yoga once a week.
twice a month
dos veces al mes
We meet twice a month.
three times a year
tres veces al año
I travel three times a year.
once in a while
de vez en cuando
I eat meat once in a while.
from time to time
de vez en cuando
She visits from time to time.
all the time
todo el tiempo
He checks his phone all the time!
04

Asking About Frequency — How often…?

Questions & natural answers
How often do you exercise?
I always go for a run in the morning. / I go three times a week.
How often do you cook at home?
I usually cook, but sometimes I order in.
Do you ever go to the cinema?
Yes, I often go on weekends. / I rarely go — I prefer streaming.
How often do you travel?
I travel twice a year. / I never travel — I love staying home!
Do you ever eat fast food?
I sometimes do, once in a while. / No, I never eat it.
How often are you late to work?
I'm never late! / I'm rarely late — maybe once a month.
05

Example Sentences

◈ always
My grandmother always says "eat before you leave the house."
Mi abuela siempre dice "come antes de salir de casa."
◈ usually
I usually listen to podcasts on my way to work.
Generalmente escucho podcasts en el camino al trabajo.
◈ often
We often go out for dinner on Friday evenings.
Frecuentemente salimos a cenar los viernes en la noche.
◈ sometimes
Sometimes the best plan is no plan at all.
A veces el mejor plan es no tener ningún plan.
◈ rarely / hardly ever
She rarely checks social media — she hardly ever uses her phone.
Ella rara vez revisa las redes sociales — casi nunca usa el celular.
◈ never
I have never tried sushi — I'm never near a Japanese restaurant!
Nunca he probado el sushi — ¡nunca estoy cerca de un restaurante japonés!
◈ Mixing adverbs
I always have breakfast, usually eat lunch, but sometimes skip dinner.
Siempre desayuno, generalmente almuerzo, pero a veces me salto la cena.
◈ End of sentence
She doesn't cook at home very often. She eats out sometimes.
Ella no cocina en casa muy seguido. A veces come afuera.

📌 Key Notes

  • Position rule 1: before the main verb → I always eat breakfast. / She never drinks coffee.
  • Position rule 2: after "to be" → He is usually late. / They are always happy.
  • Position rule 3: after auxiliary verbs (have, can, should, would) → I have always loved this.
  • Sometimes is the most flexible — it can go at the start, middle, or end of a sentence.
  • Never and rarely already have negative meaning — never use them with not: ✗ I don't never eat.
  • Other expressions (every day, once a week, twice a month) go at the end of the sentence: I go to the gym three times a week.
  • To ask about frequency: How often…? · Do you ever…? · How many times a week…?

⚠ Common mistakes:

✗ I eat always breakfast.    ✓ I always eat breakfast. (before main verb!)

✗ Always I wake up at 7.    ✓ I always wake up at 7. (always stays in the middle)

✗ I am never not late.    ✓ I am never late. (no double negative)

✗ She is usually arrives late.    ✓ She usually arrives late. (no "is" before a main verb)

✗ I sometimes am tired.    ✓ I am sometimes tired. (after "to be"!)
