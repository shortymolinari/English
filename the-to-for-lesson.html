01

THE — The Definite Article

THE = "el / la / los / las" in Spanish. You use it when both you and the listener know exactly which thing you mean. Unlike Spanish, English has only ONE form — the for masculine, feminine, singular and plural.

✓ USE THE when...
Something already mentioned — "I saw a dog. The dog was huge."Mencionado antes → ya sabemos cuál es.
Both speaker and listener know which one — "Pass me the salt."Los dos saben a cuál sal te refieres.
There is only one in the world — "the sun, the moon, the sky, the Earth"Cosas únicas en el universo.
Specific places with of — "the University of Los Andes"Nombres con "of".
Superlatives — "the best, the tallest, the most beautiful"Siempre "the" con superlativos.
Oceans, rivers, mountain ranges — "the Amazon, the Pacific, the Andes"Cuerpos de agua y cordilleras.
✗ SKIP THE when...
Countries, cities, names — "I live in Colombia." NOT "the Colombia"La mayoría de países y ciudades: sin THE.
General ideas / things in general — "Music is beautiful." NOT "the music is beautiful"Cuando hablas de algo en general.
Languages — "She speaks Spanish." NOT "the Spanish"Los idiomas no llevan THE.
Meals — "I had lunch at 1pm." NOT "the lunch"breakfast, lunch, dinner → sin artículo.
Sports & subjects — "He plays football." NOT "the football"Deportes, materias, asignaturas.
Most streets & airports — "I live on Main Street." NOT "the Main Street"Calles, aeropuertos, estaciones → sin THE.
THE
Usage examples by context
el · la · los · las — one form for everything
🎯Specific / already known
Close the window, please.
Cierra la ventana, por favor. (sabemos cuál)
I loved the movie we saw yesterday.
Me encantó la película que vimos ayer.
The president gave a speech.
El presidente dio un discurso. (el único)
Have you seen the report?
¿Has visto el informe? (uno específico)
🌍Unique things & geography
The sun rises in the east.
El sol sale por el este. (único)

The Andes are in South America.
Los Andes están en Sudamérica.
The Amazon River is the longest.
El río Amazonas es el más largo.
02

TO — Direction, Purpose & Infinitive

TO has three main uses: movement toward something, the infinitive form of verbs, and giving/sending something to someone.

TO
Direction · Infinitive · Recipient
a · hacia · para — marks movement and purpose
📍Movement / Direction — going somewhere
I'm going to the supermarket.
Voy al supermercado.
She traveled to Japan last year.
Ella viajó a Japón el año pasado.
We drove to the beach on Sunday.
Manejamos a la playa el domingo.
Turn left to get to the station.
Gira a la izquierda para llegar a la estación.
🔧Infinitive — to + base verb
I want to learn English.
Quiero aprender inglés.
She needs to call her mom.
Ella necesita llamar a su mamá.
It's great to meet you!
¡Es genial conocerte!
Remember to bring your ID.
Recuerda traer tu cédula.
🎁Recipient — giving / sending something TO someone
Send this email to your boss.
Envía este correo a tu jefe.
He gave a gift to his sister.
Él le dio un regalo a su hermana.
Can you pass this to María?
¿Puedes pasarle esto a María?
She explained it to the class.
Ella se lo explicó a la clase.
03

FOR — Purpose, Duration & Benefit

FOR expresses purpose (what something is used for), duration (how long), and benefit (who something is intended for).

FOR
Purpose · Duration · Benefit · Reason
para · por · durante — the most versatile preposition
🎯Purpose / Function — what it's used for
This knife is for cutting bread.
Este cuchillo es para cortar pan.
I bought this for the party.
Compré esto para la fiesta.
She studies for her exams every night.
Ella estudia para sus exámenes cada noche.
What is this button for?
¿Para qué es este botón?
⏱️Duration — how long something lasts
I've lived here for three years.
He vivido aquí por / durante tres años.
We waited for two hours!
¡Esperamos dos horas!
Can I borrow your pen for a minute?
¿Me prestas el bolígrafo por un minuto?
She was in the hospital for a week.
Ella estuvo en el hospital por una semana.
🧡Benefit / Intended recipient — who it's for
I made this cake for you!
¡Hice este pastel para ti!
This gift is for my mother.
Este regalo es para mi mamá.
There's a message for you.
Hay un mensaje para ti.
I'll do anything for my friends.
Haré cualquier cosa por mis amigos.
💬Reason / In favor of — expressing support or cause
Thanks for your help!
¡Gracias por tu ayuda!
I'm sorry for being late.
Lo siento por llegar tarde.
I'm for the new proposal.
Estoy a favor de la nueva propuesta.
She was fired for lying.
La despidieron por mentir.
04

TO THE — Movement to Specific Places

When you go somewhere with a definite article, you combine to + the = to the. In Spanish this often contracts to "al" (a + el).

TO THE
TO + THE combined — going to a specific place
"al" in Spanish (a + el) · used for specific destinations with articles
🛒Daily places — to the + specific location
I'm going to the supermarket.
Voy al supermercado.
She went to the doctor yesterday.
Ella fue al médico ayer.
We drove to the beach.
Manejamos a la playa.
He walked to the office.
Él caminó a la oficina.
Let's go to the movies tonight!
¡Vamos al cine esta noche!
I need to go to the bank first.
Necesito ir al banco primero.
⚠️Special cases — TO without THE
I'm going to school. (no "the")
Voy al colegio. (función, no edificio)
She went to work early.
Ella fue a trabajar temprano.
He's going to hospital. (British)
Él va al hospital. (como paciente)
I'm going to bed. (no "the")
Me voy a dormir. (acción, no objeto)
05

TO vs FOR — The Big Confusion

Both TO and FOR can translate as "para" in Spanish — but they are NOT interchangeable. Here's how to choose:

TO = action / direction
Use TO before a verb (infinitive) or a place you're moving toward.
I went to the store to buy milk.
FOR = purpose / benefit / duration
Use FOR before a noun or -ing verb to show purpose, benefit, or time.
I went to the store for milk.
Situation TO (+ verb / place) FOR (+ noun / -ing)
── Purpose ──
Explain why I called to talk to you.+ infinitive verb: to + talk I called for some advice.+ noun: advice
Going somewhere I went to the shop to buy food.destination + purpose verb I went to the shop for food.destination + noun/thing
── Recipient ──
Giving something I gave the book to her.give/send/show + TO + person I bought the book for her.buy/make/cook + FOR + person
Making something — not used here — She cooked dinner for the family.made/created + FOR + beneficiary
── Time ──
Duration — not used for duration — I studied for three hours.FOR + period of time
Deadline/limit The report is due by Friday.(by, not to, for deadline) The meeting is scheduled for Monday.planned FOR a day
── Common expressions ──
Thanks / Sorry Thanks to you, I passed."thanks to" = credit Thank you for your help!thank / apologize + FOR
Listen / speak Listen to the music.listen TO (not listen for) Wait — I'm listening for that song.waiting/searching for something specific
06

Example Sentences

◈ THE — specific
Did you feed the cat? The bowl is empty again!
¿Le diste de comer al gato? ¡El tazón está vacío de nuevo!
◈ TO — direction + infinitive
I'm going to Bogotá to visit my sister next weekend.
Voy a Bogotá a visitar a mi hermana el próximo fin de semana.
◈ FOR — duration + purpose
She has been studying for two hours for her English exam.
Ella ha estado estudiando durante dos horas para su examen de inglés.
◈ TO THE — specific destination
Can you take me to the airport? My flight is for 6am.
¿Me puedes llevar al aeropuerto? Mi vuelo es a las 6am.
◈ THE + TO + FOR together
I went to the pharmacy to get medicine for my cold.
Fui a la farmacia a conseguir medicina para mi gripa.
◈ THE — no article vs the
I love the music in general, but the music at that party was incredible.
Me encanta la música en general, pero la música en esa fiesta fue increíble.
◈ TO vs FOR — recipient
I sent a photo to my mom. I took it for her as a memory.
Le envié una foto a mi mamá. La tomé para ella como recuerdo.
◈ FOR — reason / thanks
Thank you so much for everything! I'm sorry for the delay.
¡Muchas gracias por todo! Lo siento por el retraso.

📌 Key Notes

  • THE = el/la/los/las — úsalo cuando ambos saben a cuál te refieres, con cosas únicas, con superlativos y con geografía (ríos, mares, cordilleras).
  • NO THE con países, idiomas, deportes, comidas (breakfast/lunch/dinner) y cosas en general: "I love music" (no "the music").
  • TO + verbo (infinitive) = propósito de acción: I went to buy milk.
  • FOR + sustantivo/-ing = propósito/beneficiario: I went for milk. / I bought it for her.
  • FOR + período de tiempo = duración: for three hours, for two weeks, for a long time.
  • TO THE = al (a + el) en español — siempre cuando vas a un lugar con artículo: to the store, to the beach, to the doctor.
  • Expresiones sin THE: go to school · go to work · go to bed · go to hospital (función, no edificio)
  • Expresiones fijas: thank you FOR · sorry FOR · listen TO · talk TO · give TO · make FOR

⚠ Common mistakes:

✗ I study for improve my English.    ✓ I study to improve my English. (verb → TO)

✗ She went to the school.    ✓ She went to school. (function, not building)

✗ Thanks for come.    ✓ Thanks for coming. (FOR + -ing, not base verb)

✗ I live in the Colombia.    ✓ I live in Colombia. (countries → no THE)

✗ I waited to two hours.    ✓ I waited for two hours. (duration → FOR)

✗ This is a gift to you.    ✓ This is a gift for you. (benefit → FOR)
