-ED vs -ING
-ed = how YOU feel -ing = what CAUSES the feeling

Adjectives derived from verbs — the difference that changes everything

00

The Core Rule — Feel vs Cause

-ED
How a PERSON feels
Describes the emotion or state of a person (or animal). The subject receives the feeling.
😊😴😱
I am bored.
Yo estoy aburrido. (yo siento el aburrimiento)
She felt excited.
Ella se sintió emocionada.
They were confused.
Ellos estaban confundidos.
He looked exhausted.
Él lucía agotado.
I'm interested in this topic.
Me interesa este tema.
-ING
What CAUSES the feeling
Describes the thing, situation or person that produces the emotion. The subject generates the feeling.
📚🎢😮
The class is boring.
La clase es aburrida. (la clase causa el aburrimiento)
The news was exciting.
La noticia fue emocionante.
The instructions were confusing.
Las instrucciones eran confusas.
The hike was exhausting.
La caminata fue agotadora.
It's an interesting topic.
Es un tema interesante.
🧠 Memory Trick
Ask yourself: WHO or WHAT?  → If a person feels it, use -ED.  → If a thing/situation causes it, use -ING.

02

Same Word — Two Meanings

See how the same root word creates completely different meanings in context:

-ED → Person feels it
→
I'm bored at this party.
Estoy aburrido en esta fiesta. (yo siento aburrimiento)
→
She was amazed by the view.
Ella quedó asombrada por la vista.
→
He is terrified of dogs.
Él le tiene terror a los perros.
→
I felt so embarrassed when I fell.
Me sentí muy avergonzada cuando me caí.
→
We were disappointed with the result.
Estábamos decepcionados con el resultado.
-ING → Thing/situation causes it
→
This party is boring.
Esta fiesta es aburrida. (la fiesta causa aburrimiento)
→
The view was absolutely amazing.
La vista fue absolutamente asombrosa.
→
That dog looks terrifying!
¡Ese perro parece aterrador!
→
Falling in public is embarrassing.
Caerse en público es vergonzoso.
→
The result was disappointing.
El resultado fue decepcionante.
03

Example Sentences

-ED · Person
I'm so tired after that tiring workout.
Estoy muy cansado después de ese entrenamiento agotador.
-ING · Situation
The movie was terrifying — I was terrified!
La película era aterradora — ¡yo estaba aterrado!
-ED · Person
She looked confused when I explained the rule.
Ella parecía confundida cuando expliqué la regla.
-ING · Situation
English grammar can be very confusing at first.
La gramática inglesa puede ser muy confusa al principio.
-ED + -ING in one sentence
I was fascinated by the fascinating documentary about Colombia.
Quedé fascinado por el documental fascinante sobre Colombia.
-ED + -ING in one sentence
She felt motivated after watching that motivating speech.
Ella se sintió motivada después de ver ese discurso motivador.
-ED · Person
We were amazed by the street food in Medellín.
Quedamos asombrados por la comida callejera en Medellín.
-ING · Situation
The traffic here is really annoying and overwhelming.
El tráfico aquí es muy molesto y abrumador.

📌 Key Notes

  • -ED adjectives describe how a person (or animal) feels → always used with people as subject.
  • -ING adjectives describe a thing, situation or person that causes the feeling → describes the source.
  • Common verbs with both forms: bore · excite · interest · tire · confuse · amaze · surprise · frighten · shock · disappoint · frustrate · satisfy · relax · annoy · worry · fascinate · inspire
  • Both can be used in the same sentence: "I was bored because the class was boring."
  • With to be, seem, look, feel, appear → both forms work, just check the subject: person (-ed) or thing (-ing).

⚠ Most common mistakes:

✗ The movie was bored.    ✓ The movie was boring. (movies don't feel!)

✗ I was very interesting in the book.    ✓ I was very interested in the book.

✗ He is a boring person. can be correct → but only if he CAUSES boredom in others!

✗ The news is very worrying — wait, this is ✓! News can cause worry.
