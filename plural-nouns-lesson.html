Same Singular = Plural No Change!

These words look exactly the same in singular and plural — the context tells you which one:

sheep
one sheep · five sheep
oveja / ovejas
fish
a fish · many fish
pez / peces
deer
a deer · three deer
ciervo / ciervos
moose
one moose · two moose
alce / alces
series
a series · many series
serie / series
species
one species · all species
especie / especies
07

Example Sentences

✦ + S
The students need their books and phones.
Los estudiantes necesitan sus libros y teléfonos.
✦ + ES
There are three churches and two beaches nearby.
Hay tres iglesias y dos playas cerca.
✦ -Y + IES
My hobbies include visiting libraries in different countries.
Mis pasatiempos incluyen visitar bibliotecas en diferentes países.
✦ Irregular
The children brushed their teeth and put on their shoes.
Los niños se lavaron los dientes y se pusieron los zapatos.
✦ + S
I have two dogs, three cats and several plants.
Tengo dos perros, tres gatos y varias plantas.
✦ Irregular — vowel change
The men and women sat on their feet during yoga.
Los hombres y mujeres se sentaron sobre sus pies durante yoga.

📌 Key Notes & Common Mistakes

  • Most nouns → just add -s: book → books, car → cars
  • After -s, -ss, -x, -z, -ch, -sh → add -es: bus → buses, box → boxes
  • Consonant + y → remove y, add -ies: city → cities · BUT vowel + y → just +s: day → days
  • Most -f/-fe nouns → change to -ves: leaf → leaves, knife → knives
  • Irregular plurals must be memorized: child→children, man→men, tooth→teeth, foot→feet
  • Same form: sheep, fish, deer — no change, ever!

⚠ Most common mistakes:

✗ childs    ✓ children

✗ mans / womans    ✓ men / women

✗ tooths    ✓ teeth

✗ citys    ✓ cities (consonant + y → ies!)

✗ two sheeps / two fishes    ✓ two sheep / two fish
