04

OSASCOMP in Action — Multiple Adjectives

When two or more adjectives appear before a noun, the OSASCOMP order must be respected. Each adjective is color-coded below:

2–4 adjectives before a noun — color shows OSASCOMP position
It's a beautiful big red car.
Es un carro rojo grande y hermoso. (en español el orden es diferente)
O: beautiful S: big C: red → noun: car
She wore a gorgeous long antique black silk dress.
Ella usó un hermoso vestido largo de seda negro antiguo.
O: gorgeous S: long A: antique C: black M: silk → noun: dress
He bought a lovely small old Italian wooden table.
Él compró una bonita mesa pequeña italiana de madera antigua.
O: lovely S: small A: old OR: Italian M: wooden → noun: table
They stayed in a cozy tiny new white stone cottage.
Se quedaron en una pequeña cabaña nueva de piedra blanca y acogedora.
O: cozy S: tiny A: new C: white M: stone → noun: cottage
She found an amazing large ancient round golden coin.
Ella encontró una moneda dorada redonda antigua grande y asombrosa.
O: amazing S: large A: ancient SH: round C: golden → noun: coin
He's a brilliant tall young Colombian musician.
Él es un brillante músico colombiano joven y alto.
O: brilliant S: tall A: young OR: Colombian → noun: musician
💡 Practical tip
In everyday English, native speakers rarely use more than 2–3 adjectives before a noun. More than 3 starts to sound literary or unusual. Always put a comma between adjectives of the same category (two opinion adjectives): "a lovely, comfortable sofa" — but no comma between adjectives of different categories.

Example Sentences — Describing Everything

Person — personality
My best friend is kind, funny and incredibly generous — I'm lucky to know her.
Mi mejor amiga es amable, graciosa e increíblemente generosa — tengo suerte de conocerla.
Object — OSASCOMP
He gave me a beautiful old French leather wallet for my birthday.
Me dio una hermosa cartera francesa de cuero antigua para mi cumpleaños.
Place — atmosphere
The restaurant was cozy and vibrant — the food was amazing too.
El restaurante era acogedor y animado — la comida también era increíble.
Person — physical + emotion
The tall, bearded man looked nervous and exhausted.
El hombre alto y barbudo parecía nervioso y agotado.
Object — texture + condition
The rough, rusty door looked fragile and dangerous.
La puerta áspera y oxidada parecía frágil y peligrosa.
Mixed — full OSASCOMP
She drives a gorgeous small vintage turquoise Italian car.
Ella maneja un precioso carrito italiano vintage turquesa pequeño.

📌 Key Notes — Adjectives & OSASCOMP

  • Adjectives in English are invariable — they NEVER change for gender or number: a tall man · two tall men · a tall woman (always "tall", never "talls" or "talla")
  • Adjectives go BEFORE the noun (opposite of Spanish): a red car NOT a car red
  • BUT adjectives go after "to be" (predicative): The car is red. · She is kind.
  • OSASCOMP order: Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Purpose
  • You don't need to use all 8 categories — just respect the order of the ones you DO use.
  • In practice, 2–3 adjectives before a noun is the most common in natural English. More than 3 feels literary or unusual.
  • Use a comma between two adjectives of the same category: a lovely, comfortable sofa. No comma between different categories: a lovely old house.
  • Opinions always come first because they are the most subjective — the speaker's personal view goes closest to the speaker.

⚠ Common OSASCOMP mistakes:

✗ a wooden beautiful old table    ✓ a beautiful old wooden table (Opinion first, Material last)

✗ a red big car    ✓ a big red car (Size before Color)

✗ an Italian lovely small café    ✓ a lovely small Italian café (Opinion → Size → Origin)

✗ a new beautiful French perfume    ✓ a beautiful new French perfume (Opinion → Age → Origin)

✗ She is a talls woman.    ✓ She is a tall woman. (adjectives never add -s!)
