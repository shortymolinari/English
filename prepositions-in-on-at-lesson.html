IN / ON / AT
IN = inside / enclosed ON = surface / contact AT = exact point / location

The three most important prepositions of place — and how to choose the right one

00

The Big Picture — What Each One Means

IN
Inside / Enclosed
Something is surrounded, contained, or enclosed within something else.
📦
The cat is in the box.
El gato está dentro de la caja.
I live in Medellín.
Vivo en Medellín.
She's in the car.
Ella está en el carro.
It's in the fridge.
Está en la nevera.
We are in a meeting.
Estamos en una reunión.
ON
On a Surface
Something is touching or resting on a surface, or connected to something.
📋
The book is on the table.
El libro está sobre la mesa.
We live on Baker Street.
Vivimos en Baker Street.
I'm on the 3rd floor.
Estoy en el tercer piso.
There's a fly on the wall.
Hay una mosca en la pared.
I saw it on the news.
Lo vi en las noticias.
AT
Exact Point / Spot
A specific location, address, event or destination — where something happens.
📍
She's at the door.
Ella está en la puerta.
Meet me at the airport.
Encuéntrame en el aeropuerto.
He works at a hospital.
Él trabaja en un hospital.
We're at the party.
Estamos en la fiesta.
Turn right at the corner.
Gira a la derecha en la esquina.

04

Tricky Places — Same Place, Different Prep!

Some places can use different prepositions depending on what you mean. The meaning changes!

PlacePrepExampleMeaning
── School ──
schoolin schoolShe is in school right now.Inside the building
schoolat schoolShe's at school (studying/working).Doing school activities (functional)
── Hospital ──
hospitalin hospitalHe's in hospital. (British)Admitted as a patient
hospitalat the hospitalShe works at the hospital.General location / employed there
── Car vs Bus ──
carin the carWait for me in the car.Enclosed vehicle = IN
bus / trainon the busI left my bag on the bus.Public transport = ON (you can walk around)
── Corner ──
cornerin the cornerThe lamp is in the corner of the room.Inside a room — the corner is a space
cornerat the cornerMeet me at the corner of 5th and Main.Outside on a street — a specific point
── Sea / Beach ──
seain the seaWe swam in the sea.Inside/inside the water
seaat seaThe sailors were at sea for months.On a journey/voyage at sea (idiom)
── Bed ──
bedin bedI was in bed by 10pm. (no article)Under the covers / sleeping
bedon the bedThe cat is on the bed.Resting on top of the surface
05

Example Sentences

◈ IN
She lives in a small apartment in the center of the city.
Ella vive en un pequeño apartamento en el centro de la ciudad.
◈ ON
My keys are on the table, on the left side.
Mis llaves están sobre la mesa, al lado izquierdo.
◈ AT
Let's meet at the café at the corner of the street.
Encontrémonos en el café en la esquina de la calle.
◈ IN + ON + AT
I was at work, on the 5th floor, sitting in the conference room.
Estaba en el trabajo, en el 5to piso, sentado en la sala de conferencias.
◈ IN
There's a bee in the house! It flew in through the window.
¡Hay una abeja en la casa! Entró volando por la ventana.
◈ ON
I saw your photo on Instagram — you were on the beach!
Vi tu foto en Instagram — ¡estabas en la playa!
◈ AT
She's not at home right now. She's at the gym.
Ella no está en casa ahora. Está en el gimnasio.
◈ IN + ON + AT
We're on the bus, stuck in traffic. We'll be at the station in 20 minutes.
Estamos en el bus, atascados en el tráfico. Estaremos en la estación en 20 minutos.

📌 Key Notes

  • IN = dentro de un espacio cerrado → rooms, buildings, cities, countries, water: in the kitchen · in Colombia · in the pool
  • ON = sobre una superficie o conectado a algo → surfaces, streets, floors, public transport, screens: on the table · on the bus · on TV
  • AT = punto exacto o destino → addresses, events, checkpoints, functional places: at the airport · at school · at the corner
  • Enclosed vehicles (car, taxi) → IN  ·  Public transport (bus, train, plane, metro) → ON
  • at home · at work · at school — NO article needed in these common expressions!
  • Corner inside a room → in the corner  ·  Corner of a street → at the corner
  • in bed (sleeping, under covers) vs on the bed (on top of the surface)

⚠ Most common mistakes:

✗ She is on the kitchen.    ✓ She is in the kitchen. (rooms = IN)

✗ I live at Colombia.    ✓ I live in Colombia. (countries = IN)

✗ He is in the bus.    ✓ He is on the bus. (public transport = ON)

✗ Meet me in the corner of the street.    ✓ Meet me at the corner of the street.

✗ I saw it in Instagram.    ✓ I saw it on Instagram. (social media = ON)
