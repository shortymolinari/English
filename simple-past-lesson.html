Simple Past

Actions that happened and finished in the past — Regular & Irregular verbs

✦ Regular → verb + ed ✖ Irregular → special form ? Questions → did / didn't
00

When Do We Use It?

Completed action ✓
I visited Cartagena last year.
Acción terminada, tiempo específico.
Sequence of events 🔢
She woke up, ate and left.
Serie de acciones en el pasado.
Past habits 🔁
We played football every Sunday.
Hábito pasado que ya no ocurre.
Time markers ⏱
yesterday · last night · last week · ago · in 2020 · this morning

Affirmative Sentences

Structure Subject + verb (past form) + complement
✦ Affirmative
I worked late yesterday.
Yo trabajé hasta tarde ayer.
✦ Affirmative
She went to the market this morning. irr.
Ella fue al mercado esta mañana.
✦ Affirmative
We had a great time last night. irr.
Nosotros la pasamos genial anoche.
✦ Affirmative
They played football two hours ago.
Ellos jugaron fútbol hace dos horas.
✦ Affirmative
He met his best friend in school. irr.
Él conoció a su mejor amigo en el colegio.
✦ Affirmative
I studied English last semester.
Yo estudié inglés el semestre pasado.
03

Negative Sentences

Structure Subject + did not (didn't) + verb (base form) + complement
✖ Negative
I didn't sleep well last night.
Yo no dormí bien anoche.
✖ Negative
She didn't go to the party.
Ella no fue a la fiesta.
✖ Negative
We didn't have time for lunch.
Nosotros no tuvimos tiempo para almorzar.
✖ Negative
They didn't finish the project.
Ellos no terminaron el proyecto.
✖ Negative
He didn't call me back.
Él no me devolvió la llamada.
✖ Negative
I didn't know about the meeting.
Yo no sabía de la reunión.
04

Yes / No Questions

Structure Did + Subject + verb (base form) + ?
? Yes/No
Did you enjoy the concert?
¿Disfrutaste el concierto? → Yes, I did! / No, I didn't.
? Yes/No
Did she call you yesterday?
¿Ella te llamó ayer? → Yes, she did.
? Yes/No
Did they arrive on time?
¿Llegaron a tiempo? → No, they didn't.
? Yes/No
Did he win the game?
¿Él ganó el partido? → Yes, he did!
? Yes/No
Did you eat breakfast?
¿Desayunaste? → No, I didn't.
? Yes/No
Did we meet before?
¿Nos conocemos de antes? → I don't think so!
05

WH Questions

Structure WH word + did + Subject + verb (base form) + ?
◈ WH — What
What did you do last weekend?
¿Qué hiciste el fin de semana pasado? → I went to the beach.
◈ WH — Where
Where did they go last summer?
¿A dónde fueron el verano pasado? → They went to Spain.
◈ WH — Who
Who did you meet at the party?
¿A quién conociste en la fiesta? → I met my future boss!
◈ WH — When
When did she arrive?
¿Cuándo llegó ella? → She arrived at midnight.
◈ WH — Why
Why did he leave so early?
¿Por qué se fue tan temprano? → Because he was tired.
◈ WH — How
How did you find the job?
¿Cómo encontraste el trabajo? → I found it online.
06

Real Conversation

Camila y Andrés hablan el lunes sobre lo que hicieron el fin de semana. Morado = simple past

C
Camila
Back from a trip to Bogotá
↔
A
Andrés
Stayed in Medellín
C
Hey Andrés! How was your weekend? Did you do anything fun?
¡Hola Andrés! ¿Cómo estuvo tu fin de semana? ¿Hiciste algo divertido?
A
It was great! I went to a concert on Saturday. The band was amazing! What about you?
¡Estuvo genial! Fui a un concierto el sábado. ¡La banda estuvo increíble! ¿Y tú?
C
Oh wow! Did you go alone? I traveled to Bogotá. I visited my family and we ate a lot!
¡Oh wow! ¿Fuiste solo? Yo viajé a Bogotá. Visité a mi familia y comimos mucho.
A
No, I went with my brother. We had so much fun! When did you get back?
No, fui con mi hermano. ¡Nos divertimos muchísimo! ¿Cuándo regresaste?
C
I came back yesterday evening. The bus took almost 9 hours. I didn't sleep much!
Regresé ayer en la tarde. El bus tardó casi 9 horas. ¡No dormí mucho!
A
That's a long trip! Did you enjoy it anyway? What did you do there?
¡Es un viaje largo! ¿Lo disfrutaste de todas formas? ¿Qué hiciste allá?
C
Yes! We walked around La Candelaria, tried amazing food and saw a museum. My nephew showed me the whole city!
¡Sí! Caminamos por La Candelaria, probamos comida increíble y vimos un museo. ¡Mi sobrino me mostró toda la ciudad!
A
That sounds awesome! I didn't know you had family there. Did they come to visit you here before?
¡Eso suena genial! No sabía que tenías familia allá. ¿Ellos vinieron a visitarte aquí antes?
C
Yes! My aunt visited last year. She loved Medellín! She didn't want to leave. 😄
¡Sí! Mi tía visitó el año pasado. ¡Amó Medellín! No quería irse.
A
Ha! Nobody ever wanted to leave this city! It's too good here. 😄
¡Ja! Nadie jamás quiso irse de esta ciudad. Es demasiado bueno aquí.

📌 Key Notes

  • Los verbos regulares terminan en -ed: work → worked, play → played.
  • Los verbos irregulares tienen forma propia: go → went, eat → ate, come → came.
  • En afirmativas, el verbo va directamente en pasado — sin auxiliar.
  • En negativas y preguntas, se usa did / didn't + verbo en forma base (infinitivo sin to).
  • Common time markers: yesterday, last week, two days ago, in 2022, this morning.

⚠ Error muy común — el verbo no cambia después de did/didn't:

✗ Did you went?    ✓ Did you go?

✗ She didn't worked.    ✓ She didn't work.
