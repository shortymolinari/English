Regular Verbs — The 3 Sounds of -ed

All regular verbs add -ed in the past, but the pronunciation changes depending on the final sound of the verb. There are exactly 3 possible sounds:

/t/
sounds like "T"
After voiceless sounds: /p/ /k/ /f/ /s/ /ʃ/ /tʃ/
work→worked/wɜːrkt/
walk→walked/wɔːkt/
help→helped/hɛlpt/
laugh→laughed/læft/
kiss→kissed/kɪst/
watch→watched/wɒtʃt/
/d/
sounds like "D"
After voiced sounds & vowels: /b/ /g/ /v/ /z/ /m/ /n/ /l/ /r/ + all vowels
play→played/pleɪd/
call→called/kɔːld/
love→loved/lʌvd/
open→opened/ˈoʊpənd/
travel→travelled/ˈtrævəld/
enjoy→enjoyed/ɪnˈdʒɔɪd/
/ɪd/
sounds like "ID"
Only after /t/ or /d/ sounds — adds a new syllable
want→wanted/ˈwɒntɪd/
need→needed/ˈniːdɪd/
visit→visited/ˈvɪzɪtɪd/
start→started/ˈstɑːrtɪd/
wait→waited/ˈweɪtɪd/
end→ended/ˈɛndɪd/

04

Key Notes & Tips

📌 How to Use the 3 Forms

  • V1 — Base Form: Present Simple, infinitives, after modals.  I work / She works / I can work
  • V2 — Past Simple: Actions completed in the past.  I worked / She went / They ate
  • V3 — Past Participle: Present Perfect, passive voice.  I have worked / It was eaten / She has gone
  • ⚠ read looks the same in all 3 forms but V2 & V3 are pronounced /rɛd/ (like "red"!)
  • ⚠ Verbs ending in -ed sound /ɪd/ only after /t/ and /d/ — not all -ed verbs add a syllable!
  • 🧠 Tip: Group irregular verbs by sound pattern — sing/sang/sung, ring/rang/rung, drink/drank/drunk — they share vowel changes!
