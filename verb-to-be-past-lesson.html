Negative Sentences

Structure Subject + was not (wasn't) / were not (weren't) + complement
✖ Negative

✖ Negative

Él no estaba en casa anoche.
✖ Negative

✖ Negative

✖ Negative

Ellos no estaban interesados en el viaje.
✖ Negative

04

Yes / No Questions

Structure Was / Were + Subject + complement + ?
? Yes/No
Were you nervous before the interview?
¿Estabas nervioso/a antes de la entrevista? → Yes, I was!
? Yes/No
Was she at the party last night?
¿Estaba ella en la fiesta anoche? → No, she wasn't.
? Yes/No
Were they happy with the results?
¿Estaban contentos con los resultados? → Yes, they were.
? Yes/No
Was the food good at that restaurant?
¿Estaba buena la comida en ese restaurante? → It was amazing!
? Yes/No
Were you born in Medellín?
¿Naciste en Medellín? → Yes, I was!
? Yes/No
Was it difficult to learn?
¿Fue difícil aprenderlo? → No, it wasn't.
05

WH Questions

Structure WH word + was / were + Subject + ?
◈ WH — Where
Where were you last night?
¿Dónde estabas anoche? → I was at home.
◈ WH — What
What was the movie about?
¿De qué trataba la película? → It was about time travel.
◈ WH — Who
Who was your favorite teacher?
¿Quién era tu profe favorito/a? → It was Ms. López.
◈ WH — How
How was your trip to Cartagena?
¿Cómo estuvo tu viaje a Cartagena? → It was incredible!
◈ WH — Why
Why were they so quiet?
¿Por qué estaban tan callados? → Because they were bored.
◈ WH — How old
How old were you when you started school?
¿Cuántos años tenías cuando empezaste el colegio? → I was 5.

Real Conversation

Mariana y Felipe hablan sobre el fin de semana pasado y sus recuerdos de infancia. Naranja subrayado = was / were

M
Mariana
Just back from a family reunion
↔
F
Felipe
Curious about her weekend
M
Hey Felipe! Were you at work all weekend?
¡Hola Felipe! ¿Estuviste trabajando todo el fin de semana?
F
No, I wasn't! Saturday was my day off. It was so relaxing. Where were you?
¡No, no lo estuve! El sábado fue mi día libre. Estuvo muy relajante. ¿Dónde estuviste tú?
M
I was at my grandma's house. The whole family was there. It was so nice but a little loud! 😄
Estuve en casa de mi abuela. Toda la familia estaba allí. ¡Estuvo muy bonito pero un poco ruidoso!
F
Ha! Were your cousins there too? How old were the little ones?
¡Ja! ¿Estaban tus primos también? ¿Cuántos años tenían los pequeños?
M
Yes! The youngest was only 3. They were so energetic! I was exhausted by the end. Was your weekend quiet?
¡Sí! El más pequeño tenía solo 3. ¡Estaban muy activos! Estaba agotada al final. ¿Fue tranquilo tu fin de semana?
F
It was until Sunday. Then the neighbors were super loud at night. I wasn't happy about that!
Lo fue hasta el domingo. Luego los vecinos estuvieron muy ruidosos de noche. ¡No estaba nada contento con eso!
M
Oh no! Were you able to sleep? By the way, where were you born? You once told me but I forgot.
¡Oh no! ¿Pudiste dormir? Por cierto, ¿dónde naciste? Una vez me lo dijiste pero lo olvidé.
F
I was born in Cali! But my family was from Manizales originally. We were in Cali until I was 8.
¡Nací en Cali! Pero mi familia era originalmente de Manizales. Estuvimos en Cali hasta que yo tenía 8 años.
M
Oh nice! Was it hard to move? Were you scared when you were little?
¡Qué chévere! ¿Fue difícil mudarse? ¿Tenías miedo cuando eras pequeño?
F
A little! But the new city was amazing and the people were so warm. I wasn't sad for long. 😊
¡Un poco! Pero la nueva ciudad era increíble y la gente muy cálida. No estuve triste por mucho tiempo.

📌 Key Notes

  • was → I, He, She, It (singular)  |  were → You, We, They (plural)
  • En preguntas Yes/No, el verbo va primero: Was she...? → Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.
  • En preguntas WH: Where were you? — WH word + was/were + subject.
  • No se usa did con to be — el verbo mismo forma la pregunta y la negación.
  • was not → wasn't  |  were not → weren't (contracciones muy comunes).

⚠ Error muy común:

✗ Did you was there?    ✓ Were you there?

✗ She didn't was happy.    ✓ She wasn't happy.

👉 To be nunca necesita did / didn't — ni en pasado ni en presente.
